Where EV charging becomes part of your journey.
May 2026
Confidential

The Opportunity

Rangeway is building America's first hospitality-driven premium EV charging network. We guarantee climate-controlled indoor comfort at every Waystation and Basecamp, an industry first, with Trailheads delivering comfort basics that already exceed the vast majority of public charging.

We think like hotel operators, not utility companies. Hospitality isn't something you bolt on. It's foundational to how we design every touchpoint.

Independent industry research validates this approach. Ohm Analytics' Q3 2025 US EV Charging Market Report identified Rangeway among 38 emerging charge point operators representing a new generation focused on reliability and experience quality, challenging legacy networks that prioritized coverage over customer experience.

The Problem

  • Networks treat charging as a utility stop, not a destination worth making
  • Nearly 1 in 3 public charging attempts still fail (ChargerHelp, 2025)
  • No quality restrooms or comfortable waiting areas
  • Wildly inconsistent experiences across networks

Competitive Advantage

  • Indoor Comfort Guarantee: Climate-controlled driver's lounge at every Waystation and Basecamp. A categorical differentiator competitors cannot match.
  • Experience Consistency: Same standard across the network. Competitors vary wildly between parking lots and flagship lounges.
  • Hospitality DNA: We think like hotel operators, not utility companies. Driver's lounge experience inspired by Under Canvas and AutoCamp.
  • Strategic Partnerships: Exclusive Ekoenergetyka ultra-fast hardware at Rangeway-owned sites, with Carbon DC providing engineering and systems technology across the network.

Business Model

  • Charging fees at $0.50/kWh base rate, primary revenue across all formats
  • Retail and food service from Red Canyon Roasting Company cafes at Basecamps and automated retail at Waystations and Trailheads
  • Digital advertising on displays at all locations featuring tourism partners, local attractions, and brand partnerships
  • Incentives and credits from state and federal programs including investment tax credits and utility infrastructure incentives
  • Lodging revenue from Lookout bookings at Summit locations
  • Partner revenue from tourism partnerships, event sponsorships, and affiliate commissions
